This visualization shows the half-integer cosine wave Fourier series that appears in the definition of crinkled arcs.

The formula is: f(t) = √2 * ∑n=1N xn * cos((n-1/2)πt)/((n-1/2)π)

Where xn are random coefficients (shown in the legend). You can:

  • Adjust the number of components to see how the series changes
  • Toggle the display of individual basis functions
  • Regenerate random coefficients to see different crinkled arcs

This is the standard form of crinkled arcs mentioned in the text, where the basis functions are en(x) = √2 * cos((n-1/2)πx).
